About Us

Welcome to Ace Equality, an initiative led by Siyuan Yang, a passionate high school junior dedicated to championing equality for female tennis players. Our mission is to level the playing field, ensuring equal opportunities, recognition, and support for female athletes in tennis. Join us as we advocate for change, promote awareness, and strive for a fairer future in the world of sports.

Historical Background

Gender inequality has been a persistent issue in tennis. Despite advancements, female athletes continue to face significant disparities.

Wimbledon Prize Money (1968)

In 1968, the first year of the Open Era, the prize money for the men's Wimbledon champion was more than twice of the women's.

Wimbledon Equal Prize Money (2007)

It wasn't until 2007 that Wimbledon and the French Open began awarding equal prize money to male and female champions.

Disparities in Lower-Tier Tournaments

Disparities still exist in lower-tier tournaments. For example, in many Challenger events, the prize money for men is significantly higher than for women.

Average Sponsorship Earnings

Studies show that female tennis players earn approximately 20% less in sponsorship deals compared to their male counterparts.

What We Fight For

Equal Opportunities

We raise awareness of the inequalities for female tennis players and advocate for an equal work environment. We believe that all players, regardless of gender, should have the same access to resources, coaching, and competition. By promoting fairness and equality, we aim to create a level playing field for everyone.

Equal Paychecks

We fight for women to be paid what they deserve. Women in tennis often earn less than their male counterparts for the same work. We advocate for equal prize money in tournaments, fair sponsorship deals, and equal appearance fees. Our goal is to ensure that female athletes are recognized and rewarded equally for their hard work and dedication.

Fighting Stereotypes

We educate against stereotypes and misconceptions about female athletes. Many female tennis players face prejudice and outdated beliefs about their abilities and roles. By challenging these stereotypes through education and advocacy, we aim to change the narrative and celebrate the achievements and potential of women in tennis.

News and Announcements

Billie Jean King on her legendary career and fight for equal pay in women’s sports

Marking 50 years since the U.S. Open became the first major sporting event to offer equal prize money for men and women, King reflects on the significant progress made and the challenges that remain. Her efforts have paved the way for many women athletes today, highlighting her enduring influence and commitment to equality in sports.

Women that changed sport: Billie Jean King keeps serving up change - in tennis and beyond

The pioneering tennis star has used her platform to advocate for female sports and women’s rights for over 50 years. She secured equal pay in tennis, founded the Women’s Tennis Association, and continues to inspire by supporting various women’s sports initiatives. King’s enduring legacy showcases her impact as a tireless advocate for equality in sports and society.

'You can't beat the truth': Venus Williams recalls fight for equal pay

Venus Williams reflects on her fight for equal pay in women’s tennis, starting with a pivotal 2005 speech to the Grand Slam Committee and leading to Wimbledon awarding equal prize money in 2007. She highlights the influence of her mother’s teachings on justice and the importance of persistence. Her efforts continue to inspire the push for equality in sports.
